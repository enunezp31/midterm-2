Rewriting True love with Rachel McAdams

Hello, and welcome to my webpage. This page discusses how Rachel Mcadams' films hada dire effect on America's concept of love in teh 21st century. You may be surprised to find out just how much your idea of love will align with the stories expressed in her movies. I hope all of you lovebirds enjoy this page!!

Love Story #1

The Vow: How to remember Love

This movie tells the story of how a wife gets into an accident and forgets about her lover. The man cannot let go of his love, and pursues to fall in love with her all over again. Despite the conflict she feels about still being in love with an old lover, and her parents sudden interference with her relationship; the two end up together in the end as they do fall in love with eachother once more.

Love Story #2

The Notebook: Everyone wanted a Ryan

Now. who could leave out the cliche story of how Ryan Gosling became everyone's dream man. Rachel's portrayal of a long lasting relationship in this movie is how every American wishes to live their life. Despite the character's dementia in this movie, the two die together in the same bed at the end of the movie, and leaves the audience with a bittersweet sense of how it feels to be loved to the very end.

Love Story #3?

Doctor Strange In the Multiverse of Madness or Love?

This movie falls into a different feel of how love does not have to turn bitter if it ends. Throughout the film, Doctor Strange pursues the character portrayed by Rachel, but ends up reconciling that they could never work. This transformation of feelings leaves the audience feeling good at the end of the movie about how love can present itself in different ways besides romantical.
